Especially if AI is used to automate routine, repetitive, and tedious tasks (see the \u003C/span\u003E\u003Ca href=\u0022https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2023/03/the-impact-of-ai-on-the-workplace-evidence-from-oecd-case-studies-of-ai-implementation_b4c2c6ee/2247ce58-en.pdf\u0022\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EOECD\u2019s report on the impact of AI on the workplace\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/a\u003E\u003Cspan\u003E), tasks which previously human workers were paid a pittance to execute (see the International Labour Organization\u2019s report, \u003C/span\u003E\u003Ca href=\u0022https://ssir.org/articles/entry/ai-workers-mechanical-turk\u0022\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EDigital Labour Platforms and the Future of Work\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/a\u003E\u003Cspan\u003E), isn\u2019t that a net positive to free up people to do other, more interesting, more fulfilling types of thinking? If we let the AI do the many menial tasks that encompass our jobs, to cheerfully execute hours of drudgery, don\u2019t our lives become slightly more enjoyable?\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/p\u003E\u003Cp\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EThe ease of using AI to answer our every query can also lead to lazy thinking. She has no way of proving that AI was or was not used, and the answers are fairly comprehensive, so most of the students get an A.\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/p\u003E\u003Cp\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EAI can support learning, but it can also produce an answer without teaching you how to arrive at it. The process of solving a physics problem or writing an essay may be considered by many students to be tedious (Which equations? Which sources? Which arguments?). But then, what is the point of being in school or of learning?\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/p\u003E\u003Cp\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EThere is no clear way to separate full autonomy of thinking from automating parts of menial work. It is often some blend. In previous years, \u003C/span\u003E\u003Ca href=\u0022https://www.artfish.ai/p/2025-data-collection-wrapped\u0022\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EI even had AI analyze the data for me\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/a\u003E\u003Cspan\u003E. \u003C/span\u003E\u003C/p\u003E\u003Cp\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EAm I any different from the Microphone Man? Perhaps what differentiates me is that I still collected and curated the data, formulated the questions I wanted answered, and evaluated the end results? Or that the data was my own, instead of recording other people\u2019s conversations? There will always have to be some balance between automating menial tasks to free up time for rewarding endeavors, and doing the work yourself as a learning experience.\u003C/span\u003E\u003C/p\u003E\u003Cp\u003E\u003Cspan\u003EJenny, another character in Ken Liu\u2019s story, aims to counterpoint the main character\u2019s over-reliance on his AI assistant.
