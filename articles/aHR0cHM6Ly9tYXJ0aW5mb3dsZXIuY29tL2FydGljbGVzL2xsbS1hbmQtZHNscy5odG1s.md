A specification is at best a\n        starting hypothesis: the real constraints, trade-offs, and edge cases are discovered\n        iteratively, as we proceed with the implementation. We discussed this at length in an\n        earlier article, where we called it \u003Ca href=\u0022https://martinfowler.com/articles/convo-llm-abstractions.html\u0022\u003EUpfront Specification Impossibility\u003C/a\u003E.\n        The point is not that specs are worthless, but that the first one is a hypothesis to be\n        revised, never a finished blueprint. \u003C/p\u003E\n\n\u003Cp\u003E The natural response is to iterate: refine the spec, generate code, review what comes\n        back,\n        and feed what we learn into the next round.
